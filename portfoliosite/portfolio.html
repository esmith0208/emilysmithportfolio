Humans + Research Project

Original Topic Proposal Poster

Slide Deck

Project Scope: For my Generative AI class, the goal of our final project was to explore how AI and human collaboration could be leveraged to combat a world issue. My teammates and I chose to focus on the topic of misinformation, and specifically social media's role in its spread.

What I Delivered: We began by conducting in-depth research on misinformation, analyzing how it affects different groups, especially younger audiences and older adults. Using tools like SWOT analysis and generative AI, we dissected the causes and consequences of misinformation. Our solutions centered around two possible futures: one where AI flags misleading content for human review, and another where users crowdsource fact-checking. In both cases, we aimed to empower users while restoring credibility and trust in social media.

What I Learned: The design process included ideation using generative AI, as well as prototyping potential systems that could make these futures a reality. Throughout the project, we applied a creative, research-driven approach to define the problem, analyze potential impacts, and develop solutions.

Design for Brands

Original Brand Logo VS. Redesigns

Project Scope: In my Design Thinking and Principles class, this project on rebranding the Natural History Museum allowed me to deeply engage with the process of creating a flexible brand identity.

What I Delivered: Through thorough brand research, I gained insights into the museum’s history, values, and the various elements that make it unique. This research informed my design decisions as I worked to retain the brand’s core essence while introducing dynamic, flexible elements in the logo.

What I Learned: The process—from research and sketches to final digital renderings—was instrumental in teaching me how to maintain a brand’s legacy while embracing modern, creative possibilities. This project strengthened my understanding of how design can evolve without losing sight of its foundational identity, and it reinforced the importance of aligning design decisions with a brand’s story.

Planner Concept

Final Design Brief

Project Scope: For my Design Thinking and Principles class, we followed the full design process from empathy to testing to create a solution that addresses a key problem for college students: the challenge of connecting overarching course concepts with relevant readings and assignments.

What I Delivered: Based on this insight, I developed a concept planner that organizes classes, assignments, and readings by overarching concepts, making it easier for students to connect their learning. The design process involved creating personas for busy college students with demanding majors, and I used those personas to guide the ideation and prototyping phases. User testing played a critical role in refining the solution.

What I Learned: This project helped me understand how to translate research into a tangible design that addresses real user needs, with the final poster visually representing the problem, research findings, and the solution using typography and visual elements.
